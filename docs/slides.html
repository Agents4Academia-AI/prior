Prior

An open-source, auditable map of what the literature actually claims.

Grounded in primary sources — not web search. · Team 6 · Agents4Academia

Keeping up is impossible

  • The paper firehose — nobody reads it all.
  • LLMs & web search hallucinate citations — confident, unverifiable.
  • No calibrated sense of what's established vs. contested.

Researchers need to know what's been done, and whether to trust it.

Three agents over one shared graph

  • Reader — paper → atomic claims (with evidence)
  • Cartographer — claims → typed relations
  • Contributor — paper → standalone contributions (full text)
  • Navigator — question → grounded, cited answer

OpenAlex + arXiv ▶ Reader ▶ Cartographer ▶ Contributor ▶ ATLAS ▶ Navigator

Why not just ChatGPT / web / Obsidian?

  • vs. an ungrounded LLM → won't hallucinate citations
  • vs. web search → structure, provenance, a reusable graph (we trade breadth for auditability)
  • vs. Obsidian / Notion → understands claims & disagreement, not just links you drew

"Obsidian stores notes; Prior understands claims."

The vision: calibrated evidence assessment — for ML

Medicine→ GRADE
Climate→ IPCC
Biodiversity→ IPBES
ML→ nothing. Prior is that.
  • The shared substrate the cohort plugs into — citation verification, replication, review all enrich & read one graph
